History and Geography

The Upper West Side, appreviated UWS, is a neighborhood in Manhattan bordered by Central Park to the East, Riverside Park to the West, 59th St to the South and 125th St to the North. There are several smaller neighborhoods like Lincoln Square, Manhattan Valley, and Morningside Heights that are generally included in the UWS. There is an opinion that the UWS ends at 110th and North of 110th street is Morningside Heights and Harlem.

The UWS sits on a bluff overlooking the Hudson River. Broadway is a major North-South avenue that runs through the middle of the UWS. The UWS is served by 2 train lines and many buses.

Parks, Museums, and Cultural Institutions

The UWS is principally known for its two major parks: Central and Riveside, the American Museum of Natural History, The New York Historical Society, Lincoln Center, Columbia University, and the Cathedral of Saint John the Divine.

Central Park is New York's most famous park. It serves as the western border to the neighborhood. The park serves as a recreation space for neighborhood residents, other New Yorkers, and is a main tourist attraction for visitors from around the world.

Riverside Park does not have the fame of its neighborhood sibling but it serves as a place where residents and visitors can stroll, run, or play. Its the Western bourder of the neighborhood and provides sweeping vistas of the Hudson River and New Jersey to the west.

American Museum of Natural History is one of the largest and most aclaimed museums to the natural sciences anywhere in the world.

Lincoln Center is the world's largest preforming arts complex. It features several concert halls, the Metropolitan Opera, and colleges of music and dance.

Columbia Univesity is an Ivy league Univesity with a sprawling campus over Morningside Heights centered on 116th and Broadway.

The Cathedral of Saint John the Divine claims to be is the worlds largest cathedral when completed. It sits on a hill at 110th streets towering over Morningside Heights and Manhattan Valley.

Upper West Side in Culture

The UWS is known for its architecture and is frequently seen in TV and Movies; from West Side Story to You Got Mail and Seinfeld the UWS has been used as a setting.

The UWS is a primarily residential area. Much of the UWS was built in during the first half of 20th century which gives the neighborhood a Beaux-Arts and Art Deco look. The Central Park West buildings are particularly iconic.
