A Brief History

St. George is the administrative center of Staten Island, and also serves as the visitor's introduction to Staten Island. However, it is not the oldest or most historic neigborhood. Its history is fairly recent.

While it has always been difficult for St. George to feel itself a part of New York City, the local planners have found some new ways to integrate it. There are several large construction projects underway which will bring new visitors and residents to the area. They includ a large outlet mall, baseball stadium, ferris wheel and lighthouse museum clustered around the Staten Island Ferry Terminal.

Demographics

The modern day community of St. George grew up around the Quarantine Station. It included the people who worked at the station and those people who had relatives interned there. There were many Irish immigrants interned in the Quarantine Station and thus the community developed a large Irish component, centered around St. Peters Church. As the community has grown many other people have arrived and it is now quite diverse.

Points of Interest

Staten Island Ferry

The first thing that people think of when they hear the name Staten Island, is the world famous ferry. It attracts a huge number of tourists every year. However, until now, nearly all of those tourists would arrive in St. George and immediately turn around and head back to Manhattan. The opening of new attractions, now at various stages of completion, is meant to change all that.

Borough Hall and Hyatt Street

The first building that most people see upon exiting the Ferry building, is Staten Island's historic Borough Hall. Built in 1906 in the beaux-artes style, it was designed by the architectural firm Carrere and Hastings.
Behind Borough Hall is Hyatt Street, which ascends steeply up from there. Along this, the main street of St. George are the St. George Theater and the new courthouse, completed in 2014.

Lighthouse Museum

A recently completed component of the new waterfront is the Lighthouse Museum, which stands at the shoreline to the east of the Ferry Terminal.
It will soon be complimented by the Lighthouse Point project, which includes a hotel, restaurants and residential development. Phase One of that project is already under construction and appears as a large hole in the ground for now.

The Baseball Stadium

The first project to be completed in the new waterfront area is the baseball stadium which hosts the New York Yankees single A affiliate team, the Staten Island Yankees. It opened in 2002 and there were high hopes that it would lead to an upturn in the economics of St. George. However, it wasn't really enough, in itself, to positively impact the local economy. St. George needed bigger things, and now, hopefully, it has got them.
