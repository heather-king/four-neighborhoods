A Brief History

Canarsie is a neighborhood in the most southeastern park of Brooklyn that sits near Jamaica Bay. Once a fishing village in the 1800s, Canarsie quickly became more commericialzed as the fishing sources became slowly contaminated. Now, Canarsie is a middle-class neighborhood with strong West Indian influences.

The word "Canarsie" comes from the Dutch term for the native Lenape word "Canarsee" which means "fenced land" or "fort." Now, if you ask local Brooklynites whether this term still applies, they would most likely reply with a resounding "yes" as Canarsie is rather difficult to get to. This stems from it's proximity to public transportation.

Demographics

Canarsie is a predominately Black community with over 70% of the comunity represented by Carribbean countries like Guyana, Jamaica, and Haiti.

In the early 70s and into the late 90s, Canarsie was a primarily Italian-American neighborhood. Though many of the Italian-Americans have long since moved away, the "Original Pizza" pizzaria still remains...

Notable Locations & Attractions

Canarsie Pier

Seeing as Canarsie sits on Jamaica Bay, it's only appropriate for the neighborhood to have a pier! Canarsie Pier is a bustling location where you can find numerous people fishing, flying kites, barbequing, and enjoying the sunsets at dusk. There are also kayaks and fishing equipment available for rent so that you can enjoy the pier to its fullest extent.

More information regarding the peir can be found at the end of the page in the image gallery.

Parks, Parks, and More Parks!

Canarsie has a large number of parks, with the largest appropriately named Canarsie Park. Once a fairly small park, the park has undergone a large rehabilitation which helped to open up more sections of the park to the community. The park now contains a skateboard section, numerous cricket fields, baseball diamonds, hiking paths, and a myriad of other activities that all can enjoy.

If you are lucky, you might even get a glimpse of one of the many rabbits that roam the park.

Gateway Center Mall

For all your shopping needs, The Gateway Center Mall is the only desitnation you need. First opened in 2002, the center was recently expanded in 2014 to bring the total number of stores up to 48. Whether you are looking for clothing or groceries, this mall has a wide variety of stores and amenities to make your afternoon shopping trip more enjoyable.

If shopping is not your main focus, there is another cricket field across from the main section of the mall, where you can enjoy a game.
